DESIGN SYSTEM AND IxD FOR ENTERPRISE WEB APP

ROLES: DESIGN DIRECTION, UI INVENTORY, UX WRITING, INTERACTION DESIGN

Challenge

The Imagine Deployment Manager is a complex web application. With several developers and designers working on the project at the same time, inconsistencies that impacted usability were increasing daily. With developers and designers being geographically distributed and working on different parts of the application, how might we ensure consistency and usability across the product?


What we did

We decided to create a design system for the project and began by conducting a UI inventory to fully document the scope and nature of inconsistencies within the application. With an understanding of user and business goals (in this case users were mainly designers and developers working on the project), we established design principles to serve as a guide and reference for design decisions. Finally we designed and provided documentation for components and patterns and began the process of refactoring and eliminating some of the inconsistencies earlier documented.




UI INVENTORY: COLOUR

Overall, 46 colour variations were identified in the application, with 25 being shades of grey. These many variations led to inconsistencies in meaning and interpretation, as well as increased risk of potential accessibility issues.



UI INVENTORY: TYPOGRAPHY

Inconsistencies in units used (rem vs px vs %). Inconsistencies in text and paragraph styles, with some text elements unusually larger than header elements. These inconsistencies led to confusing hierarchies and potential accessibility issues.



UI INVENTORY: BUTTONS AND ICONS

Inconsistencies in colours, margin, padding and style on button elements were observed. Icons also did not demonstrate a consistent style, with some icons represented in more than one style.



UI INVENTORY: MODALS

Inconsistencies in verbiage, button pairings, use of headers, and general appearance of modals. Combination of text and background on light theme of some modals posed severe accessibility challenges.



DESIGN PRINCIPLES

After conducting a UI inventory and presenting a report to stakeholders, the next step was to establish design principles to serve as an anchor and reference for design decisions.



ACCESSIBILITY GUIDELINES

Before designing components, we put guidelines in place to ensure that minimum required accessibility standards were met.



COMPONENTS: BUTTONS

Different buttons types and their respective states were designed and styled consistently. Guidelines on how they should be used and paired was also created.
