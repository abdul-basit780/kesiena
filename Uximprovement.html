UX IMPROVEMENT FOR TRANSPORTATION MOBILE APP

ROLES: END-TO-END DESIGN - RESEARCH, UX, UI, IX AND PROTOTYPING

Challenge

The BigRoad mobile app was not designed to scale. Increasingly complex features added over time had resulted in cluttered screens and difficult navigation. With users being generally tech unsavvy, how might we improve the app user experience such that it adequately satisfies user goals, is easy to use, and keeps up with ever-changing regulations in the transport industry?


What we did

To better understand the user and business goals, we reviewed exisitng research documentation and developed personas based on research insights. We then performed heuristic reviews and evaluations of the current system to identify and prioritize usability issues. Finally we proceeded to design solutions to various critical usability issues using an iterative design process and tools such as sketching, wireframing and hi-fi prototypes to communicate design solutions.




PERSONA

We condensed research insights into personas to keep the team aligned on the key motivations, goals and frustrations of users.



NAVIGATION ARCHITECTURE

Mapping out the navigation architecture of the current system helped us visualize at a glance the screens and actions users had to go through to accomplish their goals. We also identified cases where important screens were so deeply nested as to potentially cause usability issues for users.



GOAL HIERARCHIES

With the benefit of insights from user research, we created a hierarchy of motivations, goals and objectives, where objectives are tasks users carry out within the system in order to achieve their goals. This helped us more easily identify and prioritize important user goals.



USER FLOWS

After identifying critical tasks, we visualized the user journey through the app to accomplish these tasks. The aim was figure out pain points and opportunities to provide better interactions.



UI REVIEW

We conducted a heuristic evaluation of the mobile app and documented usability issues to be tackled, especially regarding the most critical users tasks and goals.



REDESIGN

Usability issues identified during the UI review were tackled and solutions visualized using mockups and hi-fi prototypes.
