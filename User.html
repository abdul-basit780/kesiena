USER RESEARCH INTO ONLINE LEARNING EXPERIENCES OF MILLENIALS

ROLES: USER RESEARCH, UX STRATEGY, UX DESIGN

Challenge

As a completely virtual incubator for entrepreneurs, one of the key components of Jamii are learning modules, which would deliver relevant and timely content to users in areas of design, marketing, customer acquisition, etc. With our users being globally distributed, and constantly pressed for time, how might we design modules that are easy to use and engaging enough to capture and retain their attention?


What we did

Surveys, interviews, competitive analyses and studies on online instructional design were carried out to learn more about user goals and needs to be addressed. Personas, experience maps and userflows were developed based on research findings to identify key pain points to be solved by our designs and strategise on various possible design solutions. Wireframes showing key interactions and design rationale were developed to visualize our solution to the rest of the team.




DOMAIN RESEARCH

The goal of domain research was to gain sufficient knowledge in the field of instruction design, especially with regards to best practices in online learning content creation and delivery. Research utilized publicly available published papers and other online resources. Our research findings suggested that: The UX of online learning modules may be evaluated across four criteria: content, design, interactivity and usability. As much as possible a multimodal approach (visual, auditory, kinesthetic) should be employed in content delivery, as different individuals have different effective learning methods. Observed maximum threshold for user engagement with learning modules is pegged at ten minutes. As much as possible, content should be kept short and to the point. A means of interaction between learners and modules, learners and other learners, and learners and instructors or module creators should be provided.


ONLINE SURVEYS

The goal of the online surveys was to get insights into our usersâ€™ online learning habits and methods. Our key findings showed that our target users were highly socialized and connected self learners, who valued interaction and feedback as part of the learning process. They also showed a strong preference for video and short tutorials over other more elaborate methods of content delivery.



INTERVIEWS

Interviews allowed us dig further into survey responses and get deeper insights into user goals, behaviours and motivations, their offline and online learning habits and how they currently source for, and consume, information.



PERSONAS

Based on research findings from surveys, interviews and secondary research methods, we created personas to visualize our usersâ€™ key attributes, behaviours and goals. Some of the key goals which would later inform our design strategy and direction are the need to network and stay connected with other entrepreneurs and the need for information that is direct, practical, and can be put to use immediately.



EXPERIENCE MAPS

Experience maps helped us clearly identify and articulate pain points and opportunities for improvement in the online learning experience. One very evident pain is the amount of time spent seaching and evaluating learning content before a decision is made by the user as to which content will most likely meet their needs. An opportunity here would be to develop a robust search and recommendation system that can be trusted by the user, as visualized in our design drafts.



USER JOURNEYS

With the benefit of insights from experience maps, we visualized the user journey through our proposed system, highlighting their thoughts and interactions as they carried out activities to meet their goals.



WIREFRAMES

Finally, putting everything together, we sketched out and explored possible solutions and created wireframes of our most viable options. Some of the key features of our design solutions include a robust search engine and helpful recommendation system to address the key pain identified during experience mapping. We also strived to socialize the learning experience as much as possible, leveraging on one of our usersâ€™ key attributes of being sociable and well connected.
