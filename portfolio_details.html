PRODUCT DESIGN FOR B2B ELECTRIC VEHICLE WEB APP

ROLES: END-TO-END DESIGN -STRATEGY, UX, UI, IX AND USABILITY TESTING

Challenge

With government mandates on fleets to reduce greenhouse gas emissions, an increasing number of fleet managers are compelled to inquire about the viability of adding electric vehicles (EVs) to their fleet. As EVs gradually become mainstream, we identified an opportunity to provide fleet managers an easy-to-use tool to determine the viability of replacing their current fleet vehicles with electric vehicles.


What we did

We began with customer discovery interviews to validate the problem, and then validated the solution using wireframes, leading to the development of our unique value proposition. We then began the design process, laying out principles and requirements, followed by interaction and UI design. Thereafter, we conducted usability testing to establish a benchmark against which future iterations of the product would be measured, and to ensure that the product was actually usable prior to launch. Product was launched successfully to happy and satisfied users.


PRODUCT/DESIGN PROCESS

As the first designer on the team, and with a product manager who had not previously worked with a designer, the first thing I did was work with product management to define a framework for collaboration between product managers and designers, clearly showing processes, roles and responsibilities.

VALUE PROPOSITION CANVAS

Customer discovery interviews were conducted by the product manager to validate customer problems, leading to the development of the value proposition canvas showing the jobs-to-bedone and how a potential solution might eliminate customer pain and create gains.

SOLUTION VALIDATION

Wireframes were developed to validate the solution with customers to ensure that it eliminates the pains and creates the gains desired from a high-level feature perspective.

UX STRATEGY

We began the design process by formulating and documenting high level requirements and principles that would serve as an anchor for the entire design process going forward.

TASK ANALYSIS

With the design direction established, the next step was to map out the steps users needed to take to accomplish their goal of comparing their current fleet vehicles with electric vehicles for possible replacement.

TASK FLOW

We further broke down the steps into a realistic flow through the application, documenting input requirements and key design considerations.

SYSTEM INTERACTIONS

Going one step deeper, we modelled user flows and designed the interactions with the aim of exploring concrete paths taken by the user through the system, documenting use cases, user interactions with the system and system behavior in response to user interactions.

SAMPLE UI SCREEN

Home screen, clearly explaining to the user, in as little words as possible, what to expect from the application.

SAMPLE UI SCREEN

Application broken down into simple steps, collecting the required input from users required to determine the suitable electric vehicles for their fleet.

SAMPLE UI SCREEN

Based on the input data provided, the system provides detailed analysis of suitable and best-fit electric vehicle recommendations for customers’ fleets.

USABILITY TESTING

With the UI designs complete, the Electric Vehicle Suitability Assessment application was tested with sample users, achieving a satisfactory SUS score of 79.

UX METRICS

Prior to launch, we documented UX metrics (using Google’s HEART framework) that we would measure and monitor for product success.

POST-LAUNCH FEEDBACK

The EVSA was launched, first in Europe and then in North America to happy and satisfied users. We’re currently working on making improvements to the product for the next iteration.
